## Overview

Home Assistant is incredible at collecting data, and mediocre at showing it off. The built-in cards are functional, but a dashboard full of them feels like a spreadsheet. I wanted my dashboard to feel alive.

Nvision is my answer: a collection of custom lovelace cards that are visual first. Every card is theme-aware, built on HA's native web components, and fully configurable from the visual editor. No YAML required.

## The cards

### Waveform

Live power draw rendered as a glowing waveform, colored per device. You can tell at a glance whether the PC is idling or gaming without reading a single number.

![Waveform cards showing live power draw for several devices](./images/nvision/electricity-card.gif)

### Heat map

A temporal heat map for any numeric sensor. Think GitHub contribution graph, but for your house: hours across, days down, color for intensity. Patterns you would never spot in a line graph jump right out, like exactly when the fridge compressor kicks on.

![Heat map cards showing sensor history as hourly and weekly grids](./images/nvision/heatmap-card.png)

### Reactor

An ambient orb that pulses based on a sensor's value. It's less about reading a precise number and more about feeling the state of something from across the room.

![Reactor cards with pulsing ambient orbs](./images/nvision/reactor-card.gif)

### Liquid

A fill gauge where the level is literal liquid, waves and all. Battery levels, tank levels, anything with a percentage feels right at home.

![Liquid fill cards with animated waves](./images/nvision/liquid-card.gif)

### Entity overview

Every entity in an area becomes a particle floating in a constellation. Active entities glow bigger and brighter, and key sensors get called out below. It turns "what's going on in this room?" into one glance.

![Entity overview card rendering entities as a particle constellation](./images/nvision/entity-overview-card.gif)

### Circle gauge

A clean circular progress ring for when you do just want the number, without the visual noise of the stock gauge.

![Circle gauge cards showing battery level and a daily timer](./images/nvision/circle-gauge-card.png)

### Air quality

A gauge tuned for air quality sensors, sweeping from green to red so the reading is meaningful without memorizing PPB thresholds.

![Air quality gauge cards](./images/nvision/air-quality-card.gif)

## How it's built

- **HA-native under the hood.** Cards are built from Home Assistant's own elements and theme variables, so they inherit your theme instead of fighting it.
- **Visual editor first.** Every card gets a proper config editor. If you have to open a YAML file to use a card, I consider that a bug.
- **One template, many cards.** New cards start as a copy of a minimal blank card, so the whole collection stays consistent and adding a card is fast.

## Try it

Nvision installs through [HACS](https://hacs.xyz/) as a custom repository. Add [the repo](https://github.com/iRedSC/nvision), install, and the cards show up in your dashboard's card picker.
